Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 22, 2026

NextUp Services ("NextUp", "we", "us") operates the NextUp mobile app and web service, which connects customers looking for on-demand services with nearby service providers. This policy explains what personal information we collect, how we use it, who we share it with, and the choices you have.

By creating an account or using NextUp, you agree to this policy.

1. Information we collect

Account information

  • Email address and password (password is stored as a one-way hash — we never see or store your actual password).
  • Full name.
  • Phone number (optional for customers, required for providers).
  • Account role: customer, provider, or both.

Provider business information

If you register as a provider, we also collect:

  • Business name, category, and the specific services you offer.
  • Business address, city, state, ZIP, and its geographic coordinates (latitude/longitude).
  • Public description of your business.
  • Availability status (online / offline) so customers can be matched only to providers who are currently accepting work.

Location

  • When you broadcast a service request, we collect your device's current latitude and longitude so we can route the request to providers in range.
  • We only request your location after you explicitly grant permission through iOS or Android.
  • Your exact coordinates are never shared with providers who have not been selected. Browsing providers only see approximate distance (e.g. "0.8 mi away").
  • Provider coordinates are shared with customers so they can see which providers are nearby.

Service request content

  • The category, description, preferred timeframe, optional notes, and optional gender preference you include in a request.
  • Provider responses: availability, price, and message.
  • Booking history and status of each request.

Reviews and ratings

  • Star rating (1–5) and optional written review you leave for a provider. Reviews are public in the app.

Messages

  • Direct 1:1 messages between a customer and a provider, scoped to a specific service request. Messages are stored so both parties can read the conversation.

Device and push-notification data

  • Device push tokens (Apple Push Notification service on iOS, Firebase Cloud Messaging on Android) so we can notify you of new requests, responses, and messages.
  • Operating system platform (ios or android) associated with each token.

Biometric unlock

  • If you enable Face ID or Touch ID to unlock NextUp, all biometric processing happens on your device by Apple's Secure Enclave. NextUp never receives, stores, or transmits your biometric data.

Payment information

  • Providers pay a subscription fee to list on NextUp. Payments are processed entirely by Stripe. NextUp never receives or stores your full card number, CVC, or bank details.
  • We store a Stripe customer ID and subscription ID so we can display your subscription status and link you to Stripe's hosted Customer Portal to update or cancel.

Automatically collected usage data

  • Timestamps of account creation, login, requests, responses, reviews, and messages.
  • Server logs containing IP address and user agent when you contact our API. These are kept for short-term debugging and security monitoring.

2. How we use this information

  • Match customers to providers based on category, subcategory, and distance.
  • Send push notifications to providers about new requests, and to customers about new responses and messages.
  • Process provider subscription billing.
  • Show reviews and ratings so customers can evaluate providers.
  • Prevent abuse, fraud, and spam.
  • Comply with legal obligations.

3. Who we share your information with

We do not sell your personal data to anyone. We share limited information only with the service providers we rely on to operate the app:

  • Apple Push Notification service (APNs) — to deliver push notifications on iOS devices.
  • Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) — to deliver push notifications on Android devices.
  • Stripe — to process provider subscription payments. Stripe's privacy policy.
  • Google Maps / Google Places — to look up provider business information and to compute or display map content when a map is shown in the app. Google's privacy policy.
  • OpenStreetMap / Nominatim — to reverse-geocode coordinates into city names. OSMF privacy policy.
  • Render — our hosting provider, which stores our database and runs our backend API. Render's privacy policy.
  • GitHub Pages — hosts the web version of the app and this policy.

We also share limited information within the app: when you broadcast a request, matching providers see your distance, request description, timeframe, and any notes you included. Providers do not see your email, phone, or exact address until you pick one of them — at that point the address is typically shared by the customer through the in-app messaging thread.

We may disclose information if required by law, subpoena, or court order, or when we believe in good faith that disclosure is necessary to protect the rights, property, or safety of NextUp, our users, or the public.

4. How long we keep your information

  • Account data: until you delete your account.
  • Service requests, responses, and messages: retained as part of your history so you can see past bookings.
  • Server logs: up to 30 days for debugging and abuse prevention.
  • Deleted accounts: purged within 30 days of your deletion request.

5. Your choices and rights

  • Access and update your profile information at any time in the app's Profile screen.
  • Disable push notifications in iOS or Android settings; the app continues to work without them.
  • Turn off location in iOS or Android settings; you will not be able to broadcast location-based service requests until you turn it back on.
  • Cancel your subscription (providers) via the "Manage subscription" link in the Billing screen, which opens Stripe's Customer Portal.
  • Delete your account — email us at the address below and we will delete your account and associated personal data within 30 days.
  • Request a copy of your data — email us and we will provide an export of the personal information we hold about you.

If you are a resident of California, the EU, the UK, or another jurisdiction with specific privacy rights, you may have additional rights under applicable law (e.g. GDPR, CCPA). Contact us using the email below to exercise any of those rights.

6. Children

NextUp is intended for users aged 18 and older. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 18. If you believe a child has created an account, please contact us and we will delete the account.

7. Security

We use industry-standard safeguards to protect your data, including encryption in transit (HTTPS / TLS), one-way password hashing with bcrypt, and signed JSON Web Tokens for authentication. No system is perfectly secure, but we work to respond quickly to suspected issues.

8. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last updated" date above and, where appropriate, notify you in the app or by email. Continued use of NextUp after an update means you accept the revised policy.
